Why it stands out

Keelung Islet is tiny, but it reads big in the landscape. From the Keelung coast it looks like a single dramatic shape; on the island itself, the interest is in texture: cliffs, old military traces, the striped lighthouse, and changing seasonal plants.

Top sights on the island

You are not coming here for a huge itinerary. You are coming for a concentrated set of coastal highlights.

01 · Signature stop

Keelung Islet Lighthouse

The black-and-white lighthouse is the island’s visual icon. Even people who do not care about lighthouse history usually make it their main photo target.

02 · Natural texture

Volcanic cliffs and sea erosion

The island’s appeal is geological as much as scenic: rugged rock faces, exposed coastal structure, and a steep silhouette rising sharply from the sea.

03 · Seasonal detail

Wild lilies and native plants

Spring brings one of the most distinctive visual moments, when lilies and other native plants soften the otherwise hard volcanic profile.

04 · Leftover layers

Military traces and lookout feel

The island carries remnants of its former strategic use, which adds a harsher, more layered feel to what would otherwise be a pure nature stop.

History in one clean timeline

The short version: geology made it dramatic, shipping made it important, the military made it controlled, and tourism turned it into a seasonal public destination.

Long before tourism
Keelung Islet formed as part of a volcanic system and later became a striking maritime landmark off the Keelung coast.
Sacred / storied place in local imagination
Historical accounts and later summaries describe the island as a place with older local stories and a special, somewhat forbidding aura.
Japanese and postwar military use
The island later served strategic and military functions, and traces of that period still shape its atmosphere today.
1980 lighthouse milestone
The current lighthouse became one of the island’s defining modern symbols and strengthened its role as a visual marker offshore.
Tourism opening, then long repair closure
The island opened to tourism in the early 2000s, then suffered years of storm-related closure and repairs before reopening in 2019.
Today
It is now a controlled seasonal destination with visitor caps, guided or packaged boat access, and a strong conservation logic.

Tourism essentials

This is the practical layer most people actually need before deciding whether the island is worth the hassle.

Access

Most visitors reach the island through boat operators in the Bisha / Badouzi area rather than treating it like an open walk-in attraction.

Typical mainland baseBisha Fishing Harbor

Opening logic

The island is commonly open during the calmer part of the year and closed through the rough northeast monsoon season.

Typical public seasonApr–Oct

Capacity

Visitor numbers are controlled. Popular dates can fill up, especially when weather is good and flower season overlaps with weekends.

Reported daily cap1,200

Is it worth it if I only have half a day?
Yes. That is actually the strongest use case. It is much better as a compact coastal add-on than as the single centerpiece of a whole weekend.
Can I just show up and buy a ferry on the spot?
Do not count on it. In practice, it is safer to treat Keelung Islet as an operator-booked experience with weather and capacity constraints.
Who should skip the lighthouse hike?
Anyone sensitive to heat, steep steps, exposed sun, or rougher footing. The island is small, but the terrain is still open and physically honest.
What should I verify right before going?
Operator departure, sea conditions, actual opening date, visitor quota, and whether your chosen route includes landing only, hiking, or a full circumnavigation loop.

Best nearby pairings

Keelung Islet becomes a stronger outing when paired with one or two mainland stops. These are the most natural combinations.

Bisha Fishing Harbor

Practical launch point, seafood stop, and a straightforward pre/post-boat anchor.

Heping Island Park

Great contrast with the islet: accessible mainland coast, sea-eroded rock views, and easy infrastructure.

National Museum of Marine Science & Technology

A smart indoor pairing if weather turns or if you want to add a marine-education layer to the day.
