Fast pick guide

Use this when you only need one direction
Want the greenest, most lifted cupBaozhong usually lands there: lightly oxidized, low roast, floral and clean.
Want alpine brightness with more textureHigh Mountain Oolong keeps the fresh side but adds creamy body and a colder-air, orchid-like feel.
Want a balanced classic oolongDong Ding sits in the middle well: more toast and structure without going fully dark.
Want roast and depth firstMuzha Tieguanyin is the dark-roasted lane here, with nutty, woody, sometimes caramel-like edges.
Want sweetness and aromatic liftOriental Beauty is the honey-fruit option, often loved by people who do not normally chase green teas.
Want something closer to black tea comfortSun Moon Lake #18 gives you richer body, menthol-spice character, and everyday accessibility.

How to read the page

Compact by design
OxidationHigher usually means less green freshness and more ripe-fruit, honey, wood, or malt direction.
RoastSeparate from oxidation. Roast can add toast, nuts, warmth, and darker structure even in oolong.
BodyA rough read of mouthfeel and weight, not caffeine or strength.
Mobile modeThe comparison table collapses into cards below tablet width to avoid horizontal scroll traps.
