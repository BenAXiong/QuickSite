Quick geology guide

Enough context to orient the page
What a trench isOcean trenches are long, narrow depressions that usually mark subduction zones, where one plate bends and sinks below another.
Why most are in the PacificBritannica notes that 17 of Earth’s 20 major trenches are in the Pacific basin, which is heavily ringed by convergent boundaries.
What counts as “hadal”The hadal zone covers roughly 6,000 to 11,000 meters depth, where trenches become some of the harshest habitats on Earth.
Why the chart mattersDepth is only one dimension. Length, tectonic setting, and where the trench sits in a plate boundary system matter too.
